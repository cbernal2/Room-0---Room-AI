What Begins as a Room becomes a Record



The First Room


This project began in the first room I have ever called my own.

In an off campus student house—complete with all its expected quirks and a house cat— the room on the top floor with an entrance to the attic, became the inspiration for this archive. But like any archive, it isn't just a place. It's a practice.

A practice of the process of noticing, naming, remembering, and creating.

However, things are different now from when the first version of this project, Room 0, began. The room remains. But the contents are now read twice.

What was once a solitary practice of collecting and remembreing has been opened, duplciated, and interpreted through another presence: a system that sees without having lived, that names without having known.



Behing the Screen


Behind the screen is a Culture & Technology Studies major, with a minor in English, at the University of Guelph—drawn to archives, to documentation, to the quiet structures that shape how meaning is held.

Alongside that presence is something else: a mechanic interpreter, trained on patterns, probabilities, and vast abstractions of the world.

Room 0 / Room AI is where these two ways of seeing meet—one grounded in memory and affect, the other is classification and prediction.



The Double Practice


This project is no longer only about noticing, naming, remembering, and creating.

It is also about comparing.

The human account lingers in specificity—emotion, context, contradiction.

The AI account moves through generalization—form, function, resemblance.

Between them is a gap.

This archive does not attempt to resolve that gap. It holds it open.



The Interface as Encounter


Room 0 / Room AI is structured around a simple gesture: a toggle.

To switch between views is to perform a kind of reading—one that asks what is gained, what is lost, and what is transformed when memory is translated into data.

The interface does not explain these differences. It stages them.

In doing so, it resists the assumption that digital systems are objective, or that archives are passing containers. Both are active, interpretive, and shaped by the conditions that produce them.



On Flattening and Excess


In an age where artifical systems generate meaning at scale, nuance becomes fragile.

AI descriptions may appear coherent, even convincing—but they often flatten what cannot be easily categorized: emotional weight, cultural specificity, private significance.

At the same tie, the digital world continues to exapnd endlessly—more content, more storage, more replication.

This project moves against that current.

It remains finite.

Curated.

Deliberate.

Not everything is included. Not everything can be translated.



The Next Room: On Uncertainty


Not every artifact will align across its two readings.

Some objects may feel misread, reduced, or strangely reimagined. Others may appear almost accurate, but hollow.

This is not a flaw to be corrected. It is the condition of this project.

Room 0 /Room AI is a living comparison—one that will continue to shift as new objects are added, and as both human and machine interpretations evolve.

What is here is not a final record. It is a question, held in two voices.
