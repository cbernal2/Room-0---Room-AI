Dad's Memory
I was looking for this shirt! Someone gave this to me, I think Uncle Ron or Pat, when they went to Michigan. The "Big House" is the nickname of their football stadium, because it is the largest in the US. It seats just over 100,000 people.

There’s a certain weight to this shirt that they just don’t make anymore. The cotton has softened over years of wear, and the way the "Big House" lettering has cracked over time feels like a map of every Saturday spent in a stadium seat. It’s more than just fan gear to me; it’s a tactile connection to that specific energy of Ann Arbor—the cold air, the roar of the crowd, and the ritual of the game. Even though the navy has faded to a softer hue, it’s still the first thing I grab when I want something that feels familiar and grounded. It’s outlasted dozens of other shirts, becoming a permanent fixture in my rotation.
