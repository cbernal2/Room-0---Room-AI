Prophets of Rage Tshirt



Dad's Memory
This was a collab of 2 bands... Public Enemy + Rage Against the Machine. When I heard they were touring I had my buddy at work make me this shirt. I ended up going to their concert at Budweiser stage for $50! That was pre-COVID

This shirt represents a specific kind of intensity for me. It’s not just "band merch"; it’s a souvenir from a moment when the world felt particularly loud and chaotic. Wearing it feels like a bit of a statement—the bold yellow against the deep black demands a certain level of attention.I remember the energy tied to this music, that fusion of hip-hop and heavy rock that felt like a punch to the gut. Unlike some of my older, faded shirts, I’ve kept this one in pretty good shape. It hasn't been relegated to sleepwear or gym clothes yet; it still feels like "event" clothing to me. When I put it on, I’m tapping back into that rebellious, high-voltage headspace of the shows it was made for.
