Crosley Cruiser Record Player




This record player was a gift for my 16th birthday.

The gifter, somebody that I no longer know, had actually bought it from another friend, it was used but in great shape, and I have continued to maintain its condition throughout the years. That same somebody, as I mentioned, would begin a tradition of buying me records for my next two birthdays and a couple Christmases. Since my birthdayis in June, every 6 months I would gain another record in my collection.

This was a special gesture that opened my eyes to the idea of loving someone by supporting their indvidual interests and the intentions that produce traditions.

Music, in this form, isn't just something playing in the background; it's an event. This record player represents an invitation to slow down—to choose an album, drop the needle, and commit to forty minutes of sound. Its portable design suggests it has moved from bedroom to living room, or perhapes even traveled to a friend's house for an eveneing of sharing tastes. It carries the weight of a curated collection, acting as the bridge between the digital convenience of the present and the tangible, warm imperfections of the past. It is an object of social connection and private nostalgia.
